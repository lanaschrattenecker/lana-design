Pocahontas

Why Pocahontas?

I chose Pocahontas because of the profound message woven into its songs. Colors of the Wind in particular has always stayed with me — its shifting palette of sound and imagery captures both the beauty of nature and the call for empathy across cultures. The line “You think you own whatever land you land on… but the earth is only unfamiliar to those who refuse to know it” strikes me as timeless and deeply valid.
For me, the film is not just a story about love or history, but a reminder that nature is not a backdrop — it is a living voice, and one that connects us all. That perspective has shaped the way I approach design: with respect for context, with curiosity for what lies beyond the familiar, and with the belief that creativity is at its strongest when it bridges understanding rather than reinforcing distance.

My Thoughts

Working on Pocahontas was a rewarding process from start to finish. Creating the assets felt natural and enjoyable, while designing the CD challenged me in new ways since it was a format I had never worked on before. I especially loved shaping the zine and translating the songs into visual form — experiencing the lyrics in English for the first time added depth, as I had mostly known the film in German. Seeing the entire project come together in its final form is both inspiring and deeply satisfying.

Mulan

Why Mulan?

I chose Mulan because of the powerful cultural and historical weight of its story — in a time and setting where women were not expected to lead, she rises by following her heart and redefining strength on her own terms. What moves me most is not only her courage, but the way she unites duty and individuality without losing her authenticity.
For me, Mulan is more than a tale of disguise or battle; it is about identity, resilience, and the quiet force of staying true to yourself even when the world writes a different script. That message has deeply influenced how I approach design and storytelling: brave enough to challenge norms, yet always rooted in honesty and heart.

My Thoughts

Working on Mulan felt incredibly dynamic — the assets seemed to flow out of me effortlessly, and I had to restrain myself from creating even more. While focusing on the songs, my imagination quickly expanded into a larger concept, envisioning a full sports campaign with supplements, sportswear, and branding. It was a challenge to maintain focus on the planned design direction, which ultimately led me to combine both approaches: honoring the soundtrack while also exploring how its themes could translate into contemporary campaigns. This dual path made the project especially inspiring and creatively fulfilling.

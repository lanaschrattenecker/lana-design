Inside Out Emotions Poster Series

This poster series visually explores the core emotions from the movies Inside Out and Inside Out 2, along with additional feelings such as doubt, envy, embarrassment, and nostalgic ennui. Each poster uses color and imagery to represent the essence of these complex emotions.

Disclaimer: This design project was created solely for private, non-commercial purposes as a personal exercise in design and creativity. All rights to the characters, imagery, and intellectual property used belong to their respective owners, primarily The Walt Disney Company. The images used were sourced freely from the internet and are not intended for any commercial distribution or infringement.

Joy
Joy is still the radiant leader of Riley’s emotional team in Inside Out 2, though she's learning that happiness can’t solve everything. As Riley matures, Joy struggles to stay relevant amidst more complex feelings. Her endless optimism can sometimes come off as dismissive. Eventually, she embraces a deeper version of joy—one that makes space for the other emotions and finds meaning in growth, not just smiles.
Sadness
Sadness continues to bring depth and empathy in Inside Out 2, now more attuned to Riley’s growing emotional complexity. Though often overlooked, she plays a key role in helping Riley process change, loss, and disappointment. Her quiet presence fosters connection and understanding. Sadness learns that she’s not a burden—but a guide through life’s hardest transitions.
Fear
Fear returns as the skittish protector in Inside Out 2, now overwhelmed by new teenage risks—social rejection, academic failure, and existential dread. He anticipates worst-case scenarios and urges Riley to play it safe. While he sometimes paralyzes her with doubt, Fear’s warnings can be valuable. Over time, he learns to collaborate with Courage (and a more mature Joy), helping Riley take healthy risks.
Anger
Anger is louder and more explosive in Inside Out 2, now fueled by teenage injustice and frustration. Whether it's unfair rules or feeling misunderstood, he burns with the need to defend Riley’s independence. Though his outbursts can be reckless, Anger also gives Riley the courage to speak up and set boundaries. With growth, he learns to channel his fire into assertiveness rather than rage.
Disgust
Disgust returns with her signature sass in Inside Out 2, still guarding Riley from gross-out situations and social faux pas. As a teen, her role evolves—she’s now hyper-aware of trends, appearances, and cringe-worthy moments. While her judgments can be harsh, she’s ultimately trying to help Riley fit in and stay confident. Over time, she learns that true style—and friendships—come from authenticity.
Envy
Envy is a sharp-eyed new emotion in Inside Out 2, representing the desire for what others have—popularity, talent, or attention. She constantly compares Riley to her peers, pushing her to "measure up." Though she hopes to motivate improvement, her influence leads to insecurity and jealousy. Over time, Envy learns that self-worth can't be borrowed and finds her place by encouraging inspiration rather than imitation.
Anxiety
Anxiety is a new emotion in Inside Out 2, representing nervousness, overthinking, and stress. She joins Riley's mind as a teenager, aiming to protect her from unseen future dangers. Driven by perfectionism, she overplans and tries to control Riley’s actions. Her good intentions backfire, causing inner turmoil and an anxiety attack. Eventually, she learns to let go and work alongside the other emotions in balance.
Ennui
Ennui floats in as a draped, monotone teen in Inside Out 2, personifying boredom and apathy. She sprawls across the console, uninterested in most of Riley's usual joys, especially when life feels repetitive or meaningless. Though her indifference can stall Riley's growth, she also signals when it's time for change or deeper reflection. Eventually, Ennui learns to coexist with curiosity and drive, nudging Riley to find new purpose.
Embarassment
Embarrassment (sometimes confused with Shame) is a hulking, blushing presence in Inside Out 2, embodying self-consciousness and social discomfort. He rushes in whenever Riley feels exposed or judged—especially in front of her peers. Though his instinct is to hide Riley from ridicule, his oversized reactions sometimes make things worse. Eventually, he learns to step back, helping Riley laugh at herself and recover gracefully.
Nostalgia
Nostalgia is a dreamy, sentimental figure in Inside Out 2, representing longing for the past. She appears when Riley misses childhood traditions, old friends, or simpler times. Though her memories offer comfort, they can also trap Riley in wistfulness and regret. With the help of the other emotions, Nostalgia learns to cherish the past while making space for new experiences and memories.

Welcome to my Library

This is my living reading tracker—simple, honest, and curated. I use it to document what I’m currently reading and what I’ve finished. It helps me spot patterns across genres, keep notes I’ll revisit, and stay accountable to my own curiosity.

Why I keep this public? Reading informs my design thinking, writing, and research. It's a big part of my life as I visual everything I read. It helps me form my creativity und my curiosity. And I love it.

Note: This page only lists books from this year. Books from previous years aren’t shown here—you’ll find them on my goodreads.

Favorite Books of all time

The Ice Dragon by George R.R. Martin is a wintry fable about Adara, a lonely “winter child” who befriends a legendary ice dragon as war creeps across her land. With spare, luminous prose, it blends wonder and menace, asking what courage and sacrifice look like when childhood collides with a brutal world. A haunting, standalone tale that lingers like frost.

George R.R. Martin ★★★★★

A Court of Mist and Fury (book 2 of ACOTAR) follows Feyre as she grapples with trauma and a deadly bargain that draws her into Rhysand’s Night Court. Amid court politics and the looming threat of Hybern, she discovers new power, truth, and choose-yourself love—shifting from survival to agency. A sweeping mix of healing arc, found family, and slow-burn romance.

Sarah J. Maas ★★★★★

Fourth Wing by Rebecca Yarros drops Violet Sorrengail into Basgiath War College, where only dragon riders survive—and a fragile, sharp-witted heroine must outthink brutal trials and rivals. As she forges a dangerous bond and unearths a buried conspiracy, the story blends scorch-hot romance with combat, found family, and the cost of power..

Rebecca Yarros ★★★★★

Finished Books

Richtig lernen - Dein Guide für Schule und Studium by Elena Handtrack is a practical handbook that translates learning psychology into clear, usable strategies for students. It covers planning, concentration, memory techniques, mindset, and exam preparation, showing how to study smarter rather than longer. With concrete methods and examples, it helps readers build sustainable routines and achieve better results with less stress.

Elena Handtrack ★★★★☆

Die Verwandlung by Franz Kafka tells the story of Gregor Samsa, who wakes up one morning transformed into a monstrous insect and must confront the collapse of his identity, family role, and humanity itself. In stark, unsettling prose, the novella explores alienation, duty, and the quiet brutality of everyday life—making it one of modern literature’s most haunting parables.

Ali Abdaal ★★★☆☆

Feel-Good Productivity by Ali Abdaal reframes productivity as an energizing, sustainable practice built on joy rather than pressure. Drawing on behavioral science and personal experiments, he shows how curiosity, play, and meaningful rest unlock more focus and output than discipline alone. The book offers practical tools to design habits, reduce friction, and pursue goals in a way that feels both effective and genuinely good.

Ali Abdaal ★★★☆☆

Edda - Die Götter- und Heldenlieder der Germanen preserved by Bishop Brynjólfur Sveinsson, brings together the Old Norse poems that shaped the mythic world of Odin, Thor, and the Volsung heroes. Stark, symbolic, and often haunting, these lays weave creation and doom, fate and honor—forming the core sourcebook for Norse mythology and medieval Scandinavian culture.

Brynjólfur Sveinsson ★☆☆☆☆

Die schönsten Sagen der Antike by Matthias Reiner gathers classic Greek and Roman myths—Prometheus, Pandora, Perseus, and more—retold in clear, vivid language. Each tale highlights the timeless themes behind the legends—hubris, fate, love, and transformation—making ancient stories accessible for modern readers and useful for teaching or quick reference.

Matthias Reiner ★★★★☆

Creating a Brand Identity: A Guide for Designers by Catharine Slade-Brooking is a practical, step-by-step manual for building coherent brands—from research and positioning to naming, logo systems, typography, color, and rollout. Packed with case studies, briefs, and checklists, it shows how to translate strategy into visuals and manage real-world deliverables across print and digital touchpoints.

Catharine Slade-Brooking ★★★★☆

Wie Psychopathen denken offers a clear, research-based look at psychopathic traits—strategic charm, shallow affect, and reduced empathy—and how they shape decision-making. Through case studies and neuroscience insights, it separates myth from reality by explaining reward-focused reasoning, manipulation tactics, and risk-taking. The book also gives practical red-flag cues and boundary strategies to help readers recognize patterns and protect themselves.

Christopher Berry-Dee ★★★★☆

Zyklust by Nadine Spitzley reframes the menstrual cycle as a powerful rhythm for self-management, creativity, and wellbeing. In clear, compassionate language, it explains the physiological phases and offers practical ideas—from training and nutrition to planning and relationships—to align daily life with your hormonal ebb and flow.

Nadine Spitzley ★★☆☆☆

The Art of War by Sun Tzu is a timeless manual on strategy that distills warfare into principles of intelligence, deception, positioning, and timing. In concise aphorisms, it shows how winning hinges on knowing yourself and your opponent, shaping conditions before conflict, and conserving strength. Its insights apply far beyond the battlefield—to leadership, negotiation, and everyday decision-making.

Sun Tzu ★★★☆☆

Der Digital Navigator: Ein Modell für die digitale Transformation by Thomas Mohr presents a practical framework for steering organizations through digital change, linking strategy to execution with clear roles, capability maps, and measurable outcomes. It emphasizes customer-centricity, data-driven decisions, and agile governance so leaders can prioritize initiatives, de-risk investments, and build a culture of continuous innovation.

Thomas Mohr ★★★★☆

Essential Guide to Life Drawing by Barrington Barber is a clear, practice-driven primer on drawing the human figure, covering proportion, anatomy, gesture, and foreshortening. Through step-by-step demos and targeted exercises, it teaches line, tone, and shading techniques that build confidence from quick sketches to more finished studies.

Barrington Barber ★★☆☆☆

Should All Be Feminists by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie reframes feminism as common sense: the belief in social, political, and economic equality for all genders. Drawing on vivid personal stories, she shows how gender expectations limit everyone and proposes a practical, hopeful vision rooted in dignity, choice, and joy. Short, lucid, and persuasive, it’s a powerful conversation starter for home, school, and work.

Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie ★★☆☆☆

Alter Ego Effect by Todd Herman shows how to craft a purposeful persona so you can perform as your best self when it matters most. Through “fields of play,” activation rituals, and identity-linked artifacts, you separate everyday you from performance you to bypass self-doubt and limiting narratives. Packed with stories from athletes and leaders, it’s a practical toolkit for building behavior from identity outward.

Todd Herman ★★★★☆

Schamanische Heilkunst und rituelles Drama der Indianer Nordamerikas by Åke Hultkrantz is a comparative, scholarly survey of shamanic healing and ritual performance among Indigenous peoples of North America. It explores cosmologies and the healer’s role—trance, song, drum, masks, and community participation—showing how curing rites and vision-centered dramas restore balance between person, spirit, and land. Clear and respectful, it situates these practices within historical change and cultural continuity.

Ake Hultkrantz ★★☆☆☆

Anatomy: A Love Story by Dana Schwartz is a gothic Young Adult tale set in 1817 Edinburgh, where aspiring surgeon Hazel Sinnett teams up with Jack Currer, a resurrection man who supplies cadavers to the anatomists. Amid class boundaries, plague rumors, and a city obsessed with science, their partnership—and fragile romance—tests the line between ambition, morality, and the mysteries of life and death.

Dana Schwartz ★★★☆☆

Echtzeitalter by Tonio Schachinger is a sharp coming-of-age novel set at a Viennese elite school, where student Till Kokorda endures a tyrannical teacher while finding refuge and mastery in the real-time strategy game Age of Empires II. As online victories collide with offline hierarchies, the book probes power, class, and identity—and asks what “real life” means when control feels possible only on a screen.

Tonio Schachinger ★★☆☆☆

Power: Die 48 Gesetze der Macht by Robert Greene distills centuries of court intrigue, warfare, and business into 48 terse laws for acquiring, defending, and signaling power. Presented as amoral strategy, it covers tactics like concealing intentions, mastering timing, and wielding absence or unpredictability—while warning about the costs of overreach. Read as either a playbook to emulate or a field guide to resist, it offers a stark lens on real-world influence.

Robert Greene ★★★☆☆

Heimische Giftpflanzen by Gerd and Marlene Haerkötter is a richly detailed field guide to poisonous plants native to Central Europe, blending botany, safety, and culture. Clear profiles cover habitat, appearance, toxic compounds and effects, medicinal lore, and first-aid guidance—rounded out with history and mythology that show how these species have shaped folk knowledge. A practical companion for hikers, gardeners, and curious readers alike.

Gerd Haerkötter, Marlene Haerkötter ★★★☆☆

Weiße Nächte (White Nights) by Fyodor Dostoevsky is a tender novella about a solitary dreamer in St. Petersburg who falls in love over four moonlit meetings with a young woman awaiting another man. In clear, confessional prose, it probes longing, self-deception, and the ache between fantasy and reality—showing how a brief encounter can reshape a life even as it slips away.

Fyodor Dostoevsky ★★★☆☆

Aberglaube: Folklore, Mythen und Legenden aus aller Welt by D.R. McElroy is an illustrated, region-by-region guide to superstitions, myths, gods, monsters, and curious beliefs from around the world, with brief notes on their origins and meanings. Designed for easy browsing, it pairs short entries with imagery to show how old lore still shapes everyday rituals.

Mcelroy D.R. ★★★☆☆

Hunting Adeline (Cat and Mouse Duet #2) pushes Adeline and vigilante antihero Zade from obsession into open war as they target a human-trafficking ring—and each other’s limits. Told in dual POV, it blends ruthless dark-romance heat with revenge-thriller stakes as Adeline fights for agency and Zade refuses to let go. Note: contains extreme content and heavy triggers.

H.D. Carlton ★★☆☆☆
